Visible Ticks on Vegetation

Seeing ticks crawling on grass blades, shrubs, or low-hanging branches in your yard is a clear sign of a significant infestation. Ticks often perch on vegetation waiting to attach to a passing host. Identifying these areas is crucial for targeted treatment and effective control. Our inspection process specifically looks for ticks on vegetation to ensure those areas are addressed during treatment.

Ticks are ambush predators; they don't actively chase down hosts. Instead, they engage in a behavior called 'questing,' where they climb onto vegetation and extend their front legs, waiting to grab onto anything that brushes past. If you observe ticks questing on the grass, weeds, or shrubs in your yard, it indicates a substantial tick population actively seeking hosts. These are often the areas where people and pets pick up ticks. Our treatment protocols are designed to eliminate ticks in these questing zones, significantly reducing the risk of tick encounters. We apply products to the lower parts of vegetation where ticks are most commonly found, ensuring that the treatment reaches the areas where they are actively hunting. This targeted approach is more effective and efficient than broadcasting treatments over the entire yard indiscriminately. Identifying and treating these specific areas is a key component of our successful tick control strategy. Observing ticks on your vegetation is a strong visual indicator that professional intervention is needed to control the population and make your yard safe again.
